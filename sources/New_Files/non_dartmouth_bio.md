Triumph Kia Teh is President and Founder of Palaver Institute, a pan-African research organization he established in August 2025 to address systemic barriers preventing African scholars from accessing global knowledge production systems. The Institute trains African scholars through mentorship-driven research fellowships and has produced over 50 journal articles of ethnographic research across nine disciplines in its first 12 months. Palaver operates an in-house publishing arm, Palaver Press, which addresses a critical access crisis: the average African researcher earns $300-800 monthly but faces $1,626 article processing charges to publish a single paper, resulting in an estimated 1.32 million unpublished papers annually and forcing 82% of African researchers to publish in lower-tier journals not by choice but by economic necessity. Within two weeks of launching recruitment via LinkedIn, Palaver received exactly 1,001 applications from researchers across Africa and the diaspora, with the current cohort of approximately 30 researchers representing the initial phase of addressing demand among the 10,000+ scholars who lack accessible publication pathways. The Institute hosts what has become the largest African Research Conference each June, drawing 500+ in-person attendees and 200+ virtual participants, creating the continent's premier gathering space for knowledge exchange across 54 African countries.
Triumph is Co-Founder and Chief Strategy Officer of DeWise Foundation, a youth-led organization he helped establish in October 2021 that scales access and opportunity for crisis-affected youth across Cameroon. DeWise has directly served 5,000+ crisis-affected people and raised over $70,000 in competitive grants, with each dollar raised representing expanded access to energy, health infrastructure, and leadership training in communities where such resources determine life trajectories. The organization secured $30,000 from Youth4Climate Initiative and $10,000 from Davis Projects for Peace, reached finalist status in the MTN ICT Challenge, and leads a remote team of 15+ staff while mobilizing 100+ volunteers annually. These 115+ team members and volunteers each serve their own communities, multiplying DeWise's reach. The organization has produced measurable alumni outcomes including IT engineers, solar technicians, international scholarship recipients, and health sector entrepreneurs who themselves now serve as infrastructure builders in their communities.
DeWise's flagship projects demonstrate how targeted technological solutions create cascading impact. WiseCool is a solar-powered IoT cold storage custom refrigeration unit designed to provide reliable medication refrigeration at rural clinics in crisis-affected regions, projected to increase medication lifespan by 50%. The project secured over $33,000 from MTN Cameroon and Youth4Climate Initiative grants. Each functioning cold storage unit serves an entire clinic catchment area, potentially reaching thousands of patients who depend on temperature-sensitive medications including vaccines, insulin, and antibiotics. In regions where medication spoilage forces patients to travel hours to access functional cold chains, WiseCool's deployment eliminates this barrier. WiseBox developed 1,000+ solar-powered custom power banks to restore education access for displaced youth in conflict zones where hydropower outages last for months. Sponsored by the $10,000 Davis Projects for Peace grant awarded through Dartmouth's Dickey Center, each device serves a student whose education would otherwise be interrupted by lack of power for studying, charging devices, or accessing digital learning materials. The project trained 100 Climate Justice Young Leaders on climate justice, sustainable development, and leadership during hands-on project execution, with each trained leader positioned to replicate or adapt these solutions in their own communities. In December 2024, Triumph co-designed and led the National Energy Transition Fellowship with 200+ attendees and mini-grants for youth-led climate initiatives. This program was featured nationally on CRTV, Cameroon's premier national television and radio station reaching millions of households, with representatives from the Ministry of Youth and Civic Education in attendance, positioning the work within national policy conversations on energy transition.
DeWise Foundation's external recognition validates its model's replicability. WiseCool placed third nationally in Cameroon's MTN ICT Innovation Challenge, competing against innovations from across the country's 27 million people, and advanced to the Pan-African Innovation Challenge where it placed third among entries from across the continent's 1.4 billion people in Johannesburg. This progression from national finalist to continental competitor demonstrates that solutions developed for Cameroon's crisis-affected regions hold relevance for similar contexts across Africa.
From October 2019 to September 2022, Triumph served at Open Dreams Educational NGO in Yaoundé, Cameroon, progressing from scholar to National College Access Program Coordinator in a trajectory that became the blueprint for his subsequent organizational work. He began as a scholar in 2019 while fleeing the Anglophone crisis that has displaced over 700,000 people and killed thousands, arriving with no resources. By 2021 he had advanced to lead program coordinator overseeing five regional hubs serving 150 scholars nationally. Each scholar represents a family whose investment in education shapes intergenerational trajectories. Sixty percent of these scholars came from conflict zones, requiring Triumph to provide 24/7 crisis support for internally displaced students navigating the compound barriers of higher education access and displacement. He supervised 15 staff members whose work directly touched scholar families and communities, managed residential programming that achieved a 95% completion rate demonstrating that crisis-affected students can thrive when proper infrastructure exists, and led recruitment efforts that reached 4,500 students and generated 900 applications. This recruitment represented the largest college access outreach in Open Dreams' history to that point, touching thousands of families across Cameroon. Triumph designed curriculum that was adopted across all five regional hubs, meaning his frameworks shaped every scholar's preparation regardless of which hub they attended. He coordinated high-level partnerships with international embassies and Cameroon's Ministry of Higher Education, positioning Open Dreams within national policy conversations on education access. Since 2014, Open Dreams has placed 200+ scholars at 50+ elite universities globally. Triumph was selected as one of two scholars in Open Dreams' first-ever Ivy League cohort in 2022, opening pathways for subsequent scholars to institutions including other Ivy League universities. The organization named its 10th anniversary celebration "A Decade of Triumph" and its 2024 cohort "Batch of Triumph" in recognition of this pioneering achievement and his community service impact at the organization's Yaoundé headquarters.

This selection was certified by the Director of Community Life and Youth Participation and the Head of the Primary Prevention of HIV/AIDS Project at Cameroon's Ministry of Youth Affairs and Civic Education. As Community Officer, Triumph co-organized health sensitization campaigns that engaged over 3,000 youth in sustainable community action for community impact. Each youth reached represents a node in their own social networks, carrying health information to families, peers, and communities. He co-presented a National Adolescent Health Toolkit developed through extensive research by the DESERVE team to the Minister of Youth Affairs and Civic Education in Cameroon for endorsement and national distribution to DESERVE's 3,000-member youth network and thousands more across Cameroon. National distribution means the Toolkit Triumph helped develop now shapes adolescent health programming across the country's 27 million people, with particular impact on the approximately 7.5 million Cameroonian adolescents and youth who are the Toolkit's target population.

From January to August 2022, Triumph co-led the Generation to End AIDS Project, a youth-led HIV/AIDS awareness initiative funded by UNAIDS, OAK Foundation, and Restless Development. Leading a team of seven, he coordinated monthly outreach programs that reached 400+ community members through in-person education sessions drawing 50+ attendees each month and parallel social media campaigns. In contexts where HIV/AIDS stigma prevents people from seeking testing or treatment, each person reached with accurate information represents potential lives saved and transmission chains broken. The work directly supported UNAIDS policy development and statistical analysis on HIV/AIDS in Cameroon's youth populations, connecting grassroots education to national-level policy formation that shapes programming for the country's 540,000 people living with HIV.
Between January and June 2022, Triumph contributed to an agricultural education project led by fellow Aspire Institute member Faith Nchotu. The project was one of only six globally funded from over 30 proposals submitted by the 100-person cohort, bringing experiential agricultural training to seven secondary schools in Yaoundé, reaching 400+ students with a $10,000 grant. Working alongside agricultural engineers, the team delivered hands-on curriculum addressing youth unemployment by repositioning agriculture as a viable career pathway in a country where 70% of the population depends on agriculture but youth increasingly reject it due to negative perceptions. Students learned crop cultivation, sustainable farming, and agro-processing skills including organic soap-making and food processing. The curriculum was designed for teacher-led continuation after the pilot year, meaning these seven schools continue teaching agricultural skills to new cohorts of students, with each cohort representing potential young farmers and agro-entrepreneurs who can feed communities and create jobs.
